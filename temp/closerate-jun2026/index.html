Why did close rate drop after June 16?

Reviewed every sales call from the week before and the week after, plus both sales huddles.

Prepared for Dan & Brandon · June 22, 2026 · by Emil

Confidential — internal

Bottom line: The price never went up, and the closers did not revert to leading with the high-ticket offer. The drop was driven by the lead mix on the calendar (every warm/paid lead disappeared) and a pullback in discounting — not by anything wrong with the offer or the price.

  • Price unchanged: $4,800 / 4-month was the quoted entry price in both weeks. Zero prospects were told the price was rising.
  • Closers stayed on-strategy: they led with the $4–5k 4-month and offered 12-month as the upsell — the same playbook that doubled close rate earlier this year. No drift back to leading high.
  • Real causes: the week lost all of its warm/paid leads (which produced half of the prior week's closes), and discounting fell by ~half.

Q1Did the price actually go up?

No — the quoted price was identical in both weeks.

Across all 54 pitched calls, the median quoted entry price was $4,800 for the 4-month program in both windows. The 12-month / spouse options ($8,000–$9,600) appeared as upsells, exactly as before.

Metric (from call transcripts)Prior (Jun 8–15)Recent (Jun 16–20)
Median quoted base price$4,800$4,800
Base price range$4,000–8,000$3,630–5,000
Prospects told "price is increasing"1 of 380 of 16

The base price did not move. Whatever changed, it wasn't the sticker.

Q2Were the closers over-pushing the high-ticket offer?

No — they led with the 4-month in both weeks. This was the key thing to check, and it's clean.

Context: we know from earlier this year that the order you present the offer in drives close rate. Leading with the $8–10k high-ticket (Jan–Mar) closed at ~15%; flipping to lead with the $4–5k 4-month roughly doubled it to ~30% almost overnight. So the worry was that closers had drifted back to leading high.

They hadn't. The transcripts show closers leading with the $4,800 4-month as the headline in both weeks, with 12-month as an optional upsell:

Recent week: "Brandon led with PIF $4,800, then mentioned the monthly installment." · "Joel said the investment is $4,800 for a 120-day program." · "4-month headline quoted first at $4,800; year extension framed as an option."

Only one recent call presented it as a flat "$4,800 or $9,600" binary (a minor execution miss). The strategy held. Dan's instinct to opportunistically push 8–10k when it fits is intact — and it still works when the lead is warm (the prior week's one steered-to-annual call closed at $8,000).

Q3So what actually caused the drop?

Two things, neither of them the offer.

① The calendar lost every warm / paid lead primary cause

The prior week's closes leaned heavily on warm sources that simply weren't on the calendar the following week:

Lead sourcePrior weekRecent week
Meta paid ads3 calls → 2 closes0 calls
Existing buyers (LB90 dialer upsell)2 calls → 2 closes0 (1 booked, no-showed)
Cold / organic + DM-set~33 calls → 3 closes (9%)15 calls → 0
  • 4 of the prior week's 8 closes came from just ~5 warm/paid calls. The recent week had essentially none of those — the one existing-buyer call that was booked no-showed.
  • The prior week's closers also got several "lay-down" buyers (paid on the call, no objections — David K. at $8,000, Mike A. at $4,300, Justin B. at $9,600). The recent week had zero lay-downs — every prospect had a live objection.
  • Strip out the warm/paid leads and the prior week's cold-organic close rate was only ~9%. 0 closes from 15 cold-organic calls is statistically ordinary at that base rate — not a collapse.
  • This is not a top-of-funnel problem: Meta still generated ~43 leads the week of Jun 15. They just aren't converting into booked, shown closer calls. The team also flagged a wave of unqualified "Dan fans" booking calls with no real intent (Jun 18 huddle).

② Discounting was pulled back secondary

The one real "price" effect — but it's about discount discipline, not the sticker. Discounts offered fell by roughly half:

 Prior weekRecent week
Calls where a discount was offered21 / 38 (55%)5 / 16 (31%)
Price as the main objection~16%~31%

In the prior week closers proactively softened the ask (referral discounts, currency discounts, annual promos, spouse deals). After Jun 16 most calls were a flat reveal with no discount and the payment plan only offered reactively. So the effective ask went up while the sticker stayed flat — and price objections roughly doubled. Still a minority objection (spouse / "think about it" / shopping dominate both weeks), but real.

What to do

  1. Don't change the price or the offer order. Neither caused this. Leading with $4–5k and upselling to 8–10k is working and should stay the default.
  2. Get warm/paid leads back on the calendar — the highest-leverage fix. Why are ~43/week Meta leads not converting into shown calls (setter booking throughput / lag)? Restart the existing-buyer (LB90) dialer upsells — they closed 2/2.
  3. Pick a discount posture and train to it. If we're holding firmer on price to lift cart value, drill closers to defend $4,800 on value rather than going flat/quiet. The worst outcome is the current one — they stopped discounting and didn't close.
  4. Tighten setter qualification so the cold-organic calendar fills with real buyers, not curiosity "Dan fans" (DJ already reviewing ManyChat).
  5. Don't over-read 5 days. The recent window is 15 calls over 5 days, including the Father's Day weekend, during a warm-lead drought. Give it one more week with the lead mix fixed before drawing conclusions.
