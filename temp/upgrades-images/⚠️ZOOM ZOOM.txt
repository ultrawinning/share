Here's my assessment:
You have everything you need for a qualified lead scoring system. The three qualification fields that matter for your tier logic are Q5 (occupation), Q6 (location), and Q8 (income/investment). Here's the natural tier mapping:
Qualified (green): Q5 = Business owner OR Executive/C-suite OR Professional, AND Q6 = US/UK/Canada, AND Q8 = Yes willing to invest, AND Q7 = ASAP or 1-3 months.
Amber (bookable with review): One of the three criteria is soft — e.g., "Rest of world" but everything else is green, or "Professional" in Australia (your Tier 3 never-book list says Aus — that's a conflict with the Typeform showing it as an option).
DQ (don't book): Q8 = No, OR Q5 = Other, OR Q7 = Just researching.
Now here's what I need to flag:
Problem 1 — Australia is on the form but on your never-book list. The Setters Hub doc says Australia, New Zealand, and Israel are Tier 3 (never book). But the Typeform presents Australia as a top-level option alongside US/UK/Canada. That's sending a confusing signal — leads from Australia fill out the form thinking they qualify, take a call slot, and waste closer time. Either remove Australia from the form or add it to the amber tier with a high-income-job filter.
Problem 2 — "Rest of world" is too broad. Your Setters Hub has a detailed 3-tier country system (Tier A safe to book, Tier 2 book if high-income job, Tier 3 never book). The Typeform collapses all of Europe, Middle East, Asia, and Latin America into one bucket. A $300K/year tech exec in Germany gets the same tag as an unemployed person in Venezuela. You're losing qualification signal here.
Problem 3 — The Typeform doesn't ask "how did you hear about us?" The UTMs handle attribution for Path 1 (direct to form via link), but if a setter sends someone to this form, the UTMs might not carry the right source unless the setter uses a specific link. This is the setter attribution gap.
Before I spec the Marketing Tab and the setter attribution system, one last question:
These three answers determine whether we can build the Marketing Tab on existing data or need to fix the plumbing first. The worst outcome would be building a beautiful dashboard that reads from empty fields.
