Voice Note Scripts

12 voice notes for setter DM conversations. Tap scripts to edit, changes save in your browser.

Recording Order (by energy state)

Block 1 — Heavy Hitter first. Record when voice is freshest.
Block 2 — Warmth: Identity Proof, Outbound Opener, Lead Magnet Recovery, Cold Reactivation.
Block 3 — Transactional: Price Reframe, Direct Transition, Pre-Call Lock-In, Post-Link Follow-Ups.
Block 4 — Follow-ups: Ghost Nudge #1, Ghost Nudge #2. Smile in the voice. Record last.

Voice Rules

Speak like you would to an entrepreneur friend you respect. Warm, direct, peer energy — not coach voice, not sales voice. No "just curious", no "I'd love to", no "would it be helpful if". Just talk like a human.

VN 1
Warm Outbound Opener
First-touch DM to anyone showing engagement.
A — Lurker (default)
Hey — I see you lurking. Wanted to drop in and say hi. Where are you at with your health right now?
B — Story-engaged
Hey — appreciate you watching the stories. Quick check-in — what's actually on your plate health-wise right now?
C — Commenter
Hey — saw your comment, appreciate it. Where are you at right now with the energy and the body?
▸ Context
With 3.6M+ followers, you can't claim you spotted one person. "Lurking" is playful and frames it as general observation. The closing question is open ("where are you at" not "what's wrong") — comfortable place to start. Setter picks variation based on engagement type.
VN 2
Identity Proof
When they question whether it's really you.
A — Default
Ha — yeah, it's actually me. I jump in here when I can because I want to know who I'm talking to. So — where are you at with your health right now?
B — More direct
Yeah, this is actually me, not a VA. I keep an eye on the DMs because I want to know who's in my world. Where are you at right now?
▸ Context
The proof underneath the proof is the WHY — "here's why I'm in the DMs." That reveal converts skeptics. Redirect immediately with a real question. Keep it casual, almost amused — surprise, not defensiveness.
VN 3
Lead Magnet Recovery
After they grabbed a lead magnet and went silent. Highest-frequency salvage VN.
A — Feedback-led (within 24–48hrs)
Hey — saw you grabbed the [guide]. How'd you find it? Anything in there actually land, or did it miss the mark on what you were after?
B — Curiosity-led (don't know much yet)
Hey — saw you got the [guide]. What's going on that made you grab it? People don't usually reach for that one unless something's been on their mind.
C — Implementation-led (7+ days only)
Hey — saw you grabbed the [guide] a bit ago. Be straight with me — did you actually get into it, or did it land on the pile of things you keep meaning to check out?
Only use C when the gap is real — 7+ days minimum. For 3-day silences, use A or B. "Be straight with me" hits wrong if they just got busy for a weekend.
▸ Context
Don't thank them for grabbing the resource. Open with genuine interest, not a pivot to sales. Setter swaps [guide] for the actual asset name. Three doors: feedback on the content, motivation behind grabbing it, or whether they actually used it.
— the heavy hitter —
VN 4 — Record this one first
Root Cause Explanation
45–60 seconds. Lands after goal + pain + history. Makes them want the call before anyone asks.
Canonical script (multiple takes)
So let me break down how this works because it's different from anything you've tried.

Most guys who come to me — they've done the diets, the trainers, the 12-week challenges. Works for a bit, life gets busy, back to square one.

Here's why. Every program you've tried treats the symptoms. Eat less, move more, here's your meal plan. Nobody figured out why you got here in the first place.

[pause] Think about your business. Revenue drops — you don't just throw more ads at it. You find the bottleneck. Where's the system breaking down? You fix THAT, and everything else starts working.

[pause] Your body's the same thing. There's a root cause. Could be stress jacking your cortisol, could be sleep tanking your recovery, could be nutrition timing totally off for your schedule. Could be all three compounding.

So what we do on the call is find YOUR bottleneck. Not a generic plan. Once we identify it and fix it — energy comes back, weight starts moving, sleep improves, decision-making gets sharper.

That's why guys who've been stuck for years break through with us. We're not handing out meal plans. We're fixing the system failure.
▸ Structure breakdown
  • You've tried everything — validate, don't judge. "Most guys who come to me" = you're not alone.
  • Those treated symptoms — "eat less move more." Let the frustration come through naturally.
  • Business analogy — bottleneck thinking. Revenue → find the bottleneck → fix root cause. Slow down here.
  • Your body = same system — the pivot. Cortisol, sleep, nutrition timing = the levers.
  • YOUR bottleneck — personal, specific, not generic. End on "system failure."

No CTA at the end. The setter sends VN 6 (Direct Transition) right after.
— transactional set —
VN 5
Price / Cost Reframe
When they ask how much it costs. Never quote a number in DMs.
A — Contrast (default)
So it depends — not everyone's dealing with the same stuff. A guy running on 5 hours of sleep with 40 pounds to lose is a different conversation from someone who's active but can't get the nutrition right. Best thing is to hop on a call with me or one of my team so we can actually see what's going on, and we'll tell you exactly what it looks like.
B — Business-frame
Real talk — pricing without context is impossible. You wouldn't price out a project for a client without scoping it first. Same here. Best move is to hop on a call with me or one of my team, see what you're actually dealing with, and we'll lay out exactly what it looks like.
▸ Context
Price question = buying signal, not objection. Reframe: investment depends on what they need. Use A for general; B when prospect has mentioned they're a business owner. Both route to the call.
VN 6
Direct Transition to Call
The closer move. Lands after VN 4 (Heavy Hitter).
A — Systems frame (default)
Look — from what you've told me, you know what's wrong, you've tried to fix it, it keeps coming back. That's not a motivation problem, that's a systems problem. Let's get you on a call with my team to figure out the root cause — you down?
B — Mirror-led (when you have specifics)
You've been telling me about [issue] for [time] now. You're clearly smart enough to figure out generic fixes — they're not the answer. Let's get you on a call with my team and find the actual root cause. You in?
▸ Context
"Not motivation, systems" — nobody wants to be told they lack motivation. "My team" framing keeps it honest about who's on the call. Use B when you have clear context to mirror back; A when you don't.
VN 7
Pre-Call Lock-In
After they book. No-show prevention frame.
A — Day before
Hey — looking forward to your call tomorrow with my team. Block it off like a board meeting, go through any pre-call stuff, and let me know if anything changes. They're carving out time for you, so let's make it count.
B — Day of (shorter)
Hey — quick one before your call today. Block it off proper, go through anything they sent over, show up ready. We're carving out the time — let's use it.
▸ Context
Frame the team's time as investment in them — not begged for. "Block it off like a board meeting" = entrepreneur language. A is day-before; B is day-of confirmation.
VN 10
Post-Link Follow Up — 24 Hours
They got the booking link but didn't book. Assume friction, not disinterest.
Hey — did you find a time? If the slots don't work, tell me and I'll sort it out.
▸ Context
"I'll sort it out" = you remove the obstacle personally. Peer energy — a friend helping them get something done.
VN 11
Post-Link Follow Up — 48 Hours
48 hours = either tech issue or on the fence. Remove every barrier.
Hey — any issues with the calendar? Sometimes the link acts up. Just tell me what works for you and I'll book it myself.
▸ Context
"I'll book it myself" eliminates remaining friction. Blames the tool, not the person.
— follow-ups (smile in the voice) —
VN 8
Ghost Nudge #1 — Jokey / Self-Aware
First follow-up after 24–48 hours of silence. The joke breaks the awkwardness.
A — Three options (default)
Hey — so I'm either getting ghosted, you're under a pile of emails, or Instagram ate my message. All three are completely valid. Just give me a thumbs up or down so I know you're alive over there.
B — Movie trailer
Previously, on our DM conversation... I said something, you disappeared. The audience needs closure. What's the verdict?
C — Self-roast
Hey — I realize I'm now that guy who double-texts. It's fine, I've accepted it. Just let me know if you're still breathing and we're good.
D — Entrepreneur frame
Hey — I know your inbox looks like mine right now. Just floating this back up. Yes, no, or a random emoji all work — just give me something.
▸ Context
Humor does two things: breaks awkwardness and makes it socially easy to reply. A flat delivery kills these — the smile has to be in the voice. Record with the energy of texting a mate who owes you a reply. Re-record if any take sounds even slightly wounded.
VN 9
Ghost Nudge #2 — Closing the Door
Final follow-up, 5–7 days silent. The easy out increases reply rate.
A — Default
Hey — last bump from me. If the timing's off or it's just not a priority right now, totally fine, just tell me and I'll get out of your way. If it is, let's pick this up. Either way works.
B — Cleaner close
Hey — closing the loop here. If this isn't a fit or now isn't the time, all good, just say so and I won't bother you again. If it is, let's get moving.
▸ Context
Sandler "closing the file." "Either way works" = you genuinely don't need an answer, which makes them feel safe to give one. Don't send another follow-up after this — move to LTFU. Warm and matter-of-fact, not wounded.
— reactivation —
VN 12
Cold Lead Reactivation
LTFU/Cold leads — 1+ months silent. Weekly batch sends.
A — Popped into my head (default)
Hey — you popped into my head today. We connected a while back and then life probably got in the way on both ends. Where are you at now with the energy and the body? Different chapter, same goal, or you've moved on entirely... all valid answers.
B — Random check-in
Hey — random message. We talked a while back, then crickets. Not chasing you down, but wondering if anything's shifted on the health front since then. Where are you at now?
C — Specific recall
Hey — long time. We talked back when you were dealing with [issue] — wondering if any of that's still a thing or if you got it sorted. Just checking in, no pressure either way.
▸ Context
"Popped into my head" reads personal, not automated. Shifting blame to "life" removes awkwardness. Multiple-option closes signal you don't care which they pick. Use A as default; B for shorter gap; C when you remember a specific issue.

Recording Notes

  • Most VNs run 8–20 seconds. Heavy Hitter is 45–60 seconds
  • Each variation = separate audio file. Setters rotate to avoid repetition
  • Record 2–3 takes per script. Best take wins; others archived
  • Follow-ups (VN 8, 9): re-record if any take sounds wounded or pressured
  • Heavy Hitter (VN 4): pause before "Think about your business" and "Your body's the same thing"
  • Identity Proof (VN 2): casual, almost amused — surprise, not defensiveness
  • Setters text-preview before sending: "gonna send you a quick voice note"
