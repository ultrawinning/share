Voice Note Scripts

Pre-recorded voice notes for setter DM conversations. Riff on these — they're launch pads, not teleprompters.

Voice Rules

Casual, peer-to-peer, like talking to a friend of a friend at a dinner. Business analogies. Ask "why" not "what." Never preachy. Never salesy. Kill setter-speak: no "just curious", no "I'd love to", no "would it be helpful if". Just talk like a human.

VN 1
Outbound DM — Likes
Yo what's going on — saw you liking some of the posts. What's going on with you right now?
Riff context
They're lurking, probably resonating with something specific. The question is the whole point. If you want to add warmth, "glad they're landing for you" works — but keep it under 8 seconds total.
VN 2
Outbound DM — Story Viewers
Hey what's up — noticed you been watching the stories. What made you start paying attention?
Riff context
Story viewers are more engaged than likers. "What made you start paying attention" implies something changed for them — it's a turning point question, not small talk. You can acknowledge their consistency ("you've been around for a minute") but the anchor is that one question.
VN 3
How Much Does It Cost?
So it depends — not everyone's dealing with the same stuff. A guy running on 5 hours of sleep with 40 pounds to lose is a different conversation from someone who's active but can't get the nutrition right. Best thing is to hop on a call so I can actually see what's going on, and then I'll tell you exactly what it looks like.
Riff context
Price question = buying signal. Reframe: the investment depends on what they need. Feel free to use a business analogy ("you wouldn't price a project without a scope call"). Ends with the call as the next step, not a number.
VN 4
Pre-Call Motivation
Hey — looking forward to the call. Block it off like a board meeting, go through any pre-call stuff, and let me know if anything changes. I'm carving out time for you so let's make it count.
Riff context
"Block it off like a board meeting" = their language. You're not begging them to show up — you're setting the frame that this is serious time, and you're investing yours too. Can add: "I've got a tight schedule too, so I wanna make sure we use the time well."
VN 5
Call Proposal
Look — from what you've told me, you know what's wrong, you've tried to fix it, it keeps coming back. That's not a motivation problem, that's a systems problem. Let's get on a call and figure out the root cause — you down?
Riff context
Lands after goal + pain + pleasure island + urgency. Key move: "not motivation, systems." Every entrepreneur understands systems failures. Can extend with a mirror ("you said you've been dealing with X for Y months...") but keep it tight. The explanation VN (#11) already did the heavy lifting.
VN 6
It's Really Me
Ha — yeah it's actually me. What's going on with you?
Riff context
People assume DMs are assistants or bots. Confirm and immediately turn it back to them. The shorter this is, the more authentic it feels. The redirect IS the proof.
— FOLLOW UPS —
VN 7
Follow Up 1
Hey — did you get my last message?
Riff context
That's it. One line. Short = confident. If you want to soften, "might've gotten buried" works but the bare question hits harder. 24-48 hours after no reply.
VN 8
Follow Up 2
Yo — floating this back up. If timing's off, no worries, just let me know.
Riff context
Second bump — give them an easy out. The easy out actually increases reply rate because it removes pressure. Can riff with "totally get it, life gets hectic" but keep it under 5 seconds.
VN 9
Post-Link Follow Up — 24 Hours
Hey — did you find a time? If the slots don't work, tell me and I'll sort it out.
Riff context
They got the booking link but didn't book. Assume friction, not disinterest. "I'll sort it out" = you remove the obstacle personally. Peer energy — a friend helping them get something done.
VN 10
Post-Link Follow Up — 48 Hours
Hey — any issues with the calendar? Sometimes the link acts up. Just tell me what works for you and I'll book it myself.
Riff context
48 hours = either tech issue or they're on the fence. "I'll book it myself" eliminates every barrier. Blames the tool, not the person. Can add: "I know these calendar tools can be janky."
— THE HEAVY HITTER —
VN 11 — Record this one first
Explanation VN — Root Cause Process Breakdown
So let me break down how this works because it's different from anything you've tried.

Most guys who come to me — they've done the diets, the trainers, the 12-week challenges. Works for a bit, life gets busy, back to square one.

Here's why. Every program you've tried treats the symptoms. Eat less, move more, here's your meal plan. Nobody figured out why you got here in the first place.

Think about your business. Revenue drops — you don't just throw more ads at it. You find the bottleneck. Where's the system breaking down? You fix THAT, and everything else starts working.

Your body's the same thing. There's a root cause. Could be stress jacking your cortisol, could be sleep tanking your recovery, could be nutrition timing totally off for your schedule. Could be all three compounding.

So what we do on the call is find YOUR bottleneck. Not a generic plan. Once we identify it and fix it — energy comes back, weight starts moving, sleep improves, decision-making gets sharper.

That's why guys who've been stuck for years break through with us. We're not handing out meal plans. We're fixing the system failure.
Structure to riff on (45-60 seconds)
  • You've tried everything — validate, don't judge. "Most guys who come to me" = you're not alone, this is common among high performers.
  • Those treated symptoms — the "eat less move more" line. Let that frustration come through naturally.
  • Business analogy — bottleneck thinking. Revenue drops → find the bottleneck → fix root cause. Every entrepreneur has done this in their business.
  • Your body = same system — the pivot. Cortisol, sleep, nutrition timing are the specific levers. Name whichever ones fit.
  • The call finds YOUR root cause — personal, specific, not generic. "YOUR bottleneck" is the hook.

Don't end with a close or CTA. The setter sends the call proposal (VN 5) right after. This VN's job is to make them WANT the call before anyone asks.

How to Use These

  • Record each one in a natural setting — phone mic, casual, not a studio
  • Setters save as audio clips, drop at the matching conversation stage
  • Setters text-preview before sending: "gonna send you a quick voice note"
  • Re-record quarterly or when the script/flow changes
  • VN 11 is the conversion driver — if you only record one today, it's that one
