Accountability Rules

1. Own the domain, not just the task. Brandon owns the revenue operation from booked call to cash collected, including the GHL/Meta ops layer that supports it.
2. No routing to Emil. If a closer, Riley, or the agency has a question about sales ops / GHL / Meta performance, Brandon answers it, fixes it, or delegates it. Emil is the escalation path for tech and strategy, not ops.
3. Close = money received. Verbal commits don't count. Payment in Fanbasis or Stripe.
4. EOD is non-negotiable. Missing EODs = flying blind. Two missed in a week = escalation.
5. Brandon owns the number. If Joel underperforms, that's coaching. If Riley isn't ramping, that's management. The scorecard doesn't care who missed — it cares that the team missed.
6. Quantify before complaining. "Leads were bad" needs: which leads, what was wrong, data sent to DJ. No unsubstantiated quality complaints.
