What is V2?

V1 used "is it a business cost?" as the deductibility test. That's wrong per the contract. V2 uses §4.5(a)-(g) exclusively — the contract's exhaustive list of Permitted Expenses. If an expense isn't in one of (a) through (g), it cannot reduce Net Coaching Profit, regardless of being a real business cost.

Core change: "Net Coaching Profit" = Coaching Revenue (§4.4) minus Permitted Expenses §4.5(a)-(g) ONLY. Everything else is a company cost that Dan bears — it doesn't touch the coaching P&L.

§4.5 is exhaustive. "Permitted Expenses... consist exclusively of the following:" — items not in (a)-(g) are excluded by absence. This is the airtight basis for every exclusion. Don't cite specific bars (like "CEO comp") when the simpler argument is: it's not listed.

New Requirements

1. Revenue Completeness (§4.4)

§4.4 says "all merchant accounts and payment processors." V1 only has Stripe (net). V2 must:

  • Stripe: Gross revenue (not net payouts). Stripe fees become a (d) line
  • Fanbasis: Live processor — confirm if coaching revenue flows through it
  • PayPal: PayPal fees already appear in data. If coaching payments come through PayPal, that revenue is missing
  • Other: Confirm no other processor carries coaching revenue

2. Software (e) Sub-Filter

The §4.5(a)-(g) membership test alone won't catch software misclassification — software vendors look like (e) on their face. Need a sub-rule:

  • Content tools (Kit.com, Epidemic Sound, etc.) → not_permitted.content_software
  • Build/dev tooling (Anthropic, OpenAI, Make, Zapier, Wisprflow, Abacus) → not_permitted.build_tooling
  • Coaching delivery/sales/client mgmt → permitted.e.*

Test: "Does this software directly serve coaching delivery, sales closing, or client management?" If no → fails (e).

3. Non-Coaching Revenue Split (§4.9)

V1 dumped everything non-coaching into "Excluded." V2 must separate:

  • Non-Coaching Revenue — AdSense ($4,775), X Corp, sponsorship ($7,939), course payouts. Shown separately, proves the coaching/non-coaching split holds
  • Taxes — Receiver General ($190K). Not an expense, not revenue — separate bucket
  • Balance Sheet Transfers — Card payments, FX, internal moves
  • CapEx (§4.6) — TENEX LABS pending Dan election (>$10K, amortize over 4Q)

4. §4.11 Consulting Gate (Auto-Hold Rule)

External consulting/advisory requires dual written approval (Dan + Emil) before deduction. Must be a classifier rule, not just a blocker in your head:

  • Agoge Consulting → auto-hold
  • DJUniverse, AgencyScalingSecrets, Setter Company → auto-hold
  • Any new vendor in QB "Subcontract" / "Professional Development" → auto-hold until reviewed

Rule: review_status = 'Pending §4.11' → out of totals until approval logged in decision_log

Impact on Net Coaching Profit

V2 increases Net Coaching Profit. By removing ~$25K/mo in expenses that V1 was deducting (Emil's fee, legal, content/build tools), the coaching P&L gets tighter. Fewer deductions = higher NCP = higher bonus base for Emil. This is correct per the contract — those costs are real but not Permitted Expenses. Dan still bears them as company costs; they just don't reduce the coaching line of business.

Expect the March V2 P&L to show NCP roughly $25K higher than V1. This isn't a windfall — it's the contract being applied correctly.

Once the membership test runs, items that were comfortably classified under V1 (Emil's fee as "OpEx Team Labor," legal as "Professional Services") flip to not_permitted. That's the system working. The flag count will jump — that's expected, not a regression.

  • 'a' through 'g' = Permitted Expense under that section

P&L query: WHERE contract_section IN ('a','b','c','d','e','f','g') = all permitted expenses. Simple, auditable.

Revenue Completeness Checks NEW

Run monthly before P&L finalization:

  1. Sum Stripe gross revenue (API balance_transactions) — compare to QB Stripe deposits + fees
  2. Check Fanbasis for coaching payments not appearing in QB
  3. Check PayPal for coaching payments (PayPal fees exist, so payments likely do too)
  4. Reconcile: Stripe gross + Fanbasis + PayPal = total coaching revenue per §4.4

Duplicate Detection Rules NEW

QB API imports both credit card charges AND PayPal payments for the same underlying purchase. Detection:

  • Same vendor + same absolute amount + same date (±1 day) = candidate duplicate
  • Known patterns: Vimeo ($295.64 ×2), GoDaddy (doubled), Apple/Uber/Trip.com (doubled)
  • PayPal "Payment to X" + credit card charge for X = same purchase

Action: flag as review_status = 'Possible duplicate', exclude one from totals.
