How a lead moves through HPF — start to close

Every lead lives on one ladder. The setter, dialer, closer and confirm boards are just different windows onto that same ladder — not separate pipelines. This is the plain-English picture of how it all fits, and the rules that govern it.

🪜 One spine, many windows. A lead has one position on the funnel; each team sees the slice that's theirs to work.

2The two rules that govern everything

These two were locked by Emil. Every routing and clean-up decision in the system follows from them.

Rule 1 · Ownership

Whoever works the lead, owns it

The routing lane decides where a new lead lands. But the moment a person actually calls or claims it, ownership moves to them — a claim beats the lane. One owner at a time.

Example: a lead was routed to Riley by lane, but Angelica picked up the phone and worked him → he's Angelica's.
Rule 2 · Clean-up

Done = tidy up. Not-done = leave it open.

When a lead is truly finished — disqualified, lost, or won — its leftover cards on the other boards get closed automatically. When it's just a no-show or cancel, the cards stay open so someone re-works it.

Example: a lead DQ'd on the call is done → her stale card on another board is archived, not left to rot.

Two different jobs, don't confuse them: Claiming a lead = "this one's mine" (credit). Confirming a call = ringing a self-booked appointment to lock it in. Most leads should arrive already-claimed because the setter used their own booking link (which tags them automatically). When someone books manually through the shared calendar, the lead arrives untagged — the fix is to give each person a tagged booking link so manual bookings claim themselves. Anything that still self-books with no owner (mostly Email, some LinkedIn) goes to Angelica's confirm rail to lock in.

5When a call doesn't close — recovery vs. done

Not every booked call ends in a sale. What happens next splits cleanly in two — and that split is Rule 2 in action.

↻ Recovery — keep it open

The lead can still come back, so the card stays live and someone re-works it.
No-show Cancelled Follow-up booked

■ Terminal — close the cards

The lead is finished, so its cards across every board are archived automatically.
Disqualified Not interested (said no) Ghosted (went silent ×3) Won 🏆
Objections are tracked separately. Why a call didn't close — price, spouse, timing — is recorded on its own, not mixed into the outcome. That's deliberate: it's what lets us tailor training (e.g. spouse-objection handling) instead of losing the reason.
